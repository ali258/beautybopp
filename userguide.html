Step-by-step process

Preparations

Start by washing your hands thoroughly to ensure they are clean. If you have any old nail polish or residues, remove them using nail polish remover. Push back your cuticles gently using a cuticle pusher.

Selecting and preparing nail tips

Choose the appropriate nail tip sizes that fit your natural nails. Trim the tips to your desired length using nail clippers. File the tips to shape them and create a smooth edge.

Nail tip application

Apply a small amount of nail glue to the back of the nail tip. Carefully place the nail tip on your natural nail, aligning it properly. Hold it in place for a few seconds to ensure it sticks.

Applying nail forms (if using sculpting method)

If you're using forms, follow the instructions that come with the kit to apply them properly to your natural nails.

Applying the acrylic

In a dappen dish or glass container, pour a small amount of acrylic liquid (monomer). Dip your acrylic brush into the liquid and then into the acrylic powder, creating a small bead on the brush. Apply the acrylic bead to the area where the nail tip meets your natural nail or the area covered by the form (the zone called the "stress area"). Use the brush to shape the acrylic and create a smooth surface, working quickly but precisely.

Repeat for each nail

Work on one nail at a time, applying acrylic to each nail until you finish the set.

Shaping and filing

Once the acrylic has dried and hardened, use a nail file to shape and refine the nails to your desired length and style. Be gentle and careful while filing to avoid damaging the natural nail or the acrylic.

Buffing and smoothing

Use a nail buffer to smooth out the surface of the acrylic nails, removing any file marks and imperfections.

Cleaning and finishing

Use a lint-free wipe or paper towel to remove any dust or debris from the filing and buffing. Apply a nail primer to enhance adhesion (optional but recommended).
